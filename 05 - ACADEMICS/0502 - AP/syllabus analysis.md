# Institutional Framework: The NEP 2020 Paradigm Shift

Under the National Education Policy (NEP 2020), Indian universities have restructured their higher education curricula into the Four-Year Undergraduate Programme (FYUP) and revised Postgraduate (MA) structures. This framework relies on a credit-based system categorized into:
*   Discipline Specific Core (DSC): Mandatory foundational papers in the major subject.
*   Discipline Specific Elective (DSE): Advanced, specialized papers within the discipline.
*   Generic Elective (GE): Interdisciplinary papers offered to students of other departments.
*   Skill Enhancement Course (SEC): Applied, vocational, and industry-oriented papers.
*   Ability Enhancement Course (AEC): Focus on language proficiency and communication.
*   Value Addition Course (VAC): Centered on ethics, heritage, and social values.

The following academic audit maps the active curricula of the University of Delhi (DU), the English and Foreign Languages University (EFLU), Hyderabad, Jawaharlal Nehru University (JNU), the University of Hyderabad (UoH), and Jadavpur University (JU) against the 13 core academic modules.

## Module 1: British Literature

### 1. University of Delhi (DU) — BA (Hons) English (UGCF)
*   **Course Title:** Romantic Literature (DSC-7)
*   **Core Sub-Topics & Units:**
    *   **Unit I:** Romanticism as an aesthetic concept; the French Revolution and social change; poetry of William Blake ("Lamb", "Tyger", "Chimney Sweeper" from Innocence and Experience), William Wordsworth ("Tintern Abbey", "London"), and Samuel Taylor Coleridge ("Kubla Khan", "Dejection: An Ode").
    *   **Unit II:** Late Romantics; Percy Bysshe Shelley ("Ozymandias", "Ode to the West Wind") and John Keats ("Ode to a Nightingale", "Ode on a Grecian Urn", "Ode to Autumn").
    *   **Unit III:** The Romantic novel; Mary Shelley's Frankenstein.
*   **Prescribed Texts & Authors:** Mary Shelley (Frankenstein); William Wordsworth ("Preface to Lyrical Ballads").

### 2. Jadavpur University (JU) — BA (Hons) English
*   **Course Title:** The Romantic Period (Semester I, Course 1)
*   **Core Sub-Topics & Units:**
    *   **Romantic Poetry:** Charlotte Smith, Anna Barbauld, Samuel T. Coleridge (The Rime of the Ancient Mariner), Lord Byron (Beppo), and Percy Bysshe Shelley (The Masque of Anarchy).
    *   **Romantic Prose:** Mary Wollstonecraft (A Vindication of the Rights of Woman); Thomas Paine (Rights of Man); Thomas Love Peacock (Nightmare Abbey).

### 3. Jadavpur University (JU) — BA (Hons) English
*   **Course Title:** Detailed Study of a Shakespeare Play (Semester V, Course 9)
*   **Core Sub-Topics & Units:** An entire semester dedicated to a single Shakespearean text (typically King Lear, The Tempest, or Hamlet), examining early modern printing, textual transmission, folio/quarto variants, and contemporary performance studies.

### 4. Jawaharlal Nehru University (JNU) — MA English
*   **Course Title:** Elizabethan and Augustan Literature
*   **Core Sub-Topics & Units:**
    *   **Module I:** Middle English transition; Langland (Piers Plowman) and Chaucer (The Prologue to The Canterbury Tales).
    *   **Module II:** 16th-century sonnets; Wyatt, Spenser, Shakespeare, and Philip Sidney.
    *   **Module III:** Restoration and Augustan satire; John Dryden, Alexander Pope, Jonathan Swift.
*   **Prescribed Texts & Authors:** Geoffrey Chaucer (The Canterbury Tales); John Milton (Paradise Lost); Jonathan Swift (Gulliver’s Travels).

### 5. University of Hyderabad (UoH) — MA English
*   **Course Title:** Anglo-American Prose I & II (Core Papers)
*   **Core Sub-Topics & Units:**
    *   **British Non-Fictional & Fictional Prose (1830s to Present):** Victorian sage writers, the evolution of the industrial novel, and modern/postmodern British stream of consciousness.
*   **Prescribed Texts & Authors:** John Henry Cardinal Newman, John Stuart Mill, John Ruskin, Charles Darwin, Robert Louis Stevenson, Virginia Woolf, James Joyce, George Orwell.

### NEP 2020 Structural Changes
Traditional British Literature remains the largest core element but is no longer structurally continuous. Universities have broken these long-duration historical surveys (e.g., DU's old "14th to 17th Century Poetry and Drama") into modular papers focusing on specific aesthetic movements, allowing students exiting with a 1-year Certificate or 2-year Diploma to complete cohesive thematic units.

## Module 2: American Literature

| University | BA (Hons) / Undergraduate | Master of Arts (MA) |
|---|---|---|
| DU | "American Literature" (Core DSC) | "Anglo-American Prose" (Core) |
| EFLU | "American Literature" (BAENGLITE355) | "American Literature & Culture" (Core) |
| UoH | "American Literature" (Core) | "Jewish/African-American Lit" (Elective) |

### 1. English and Foreign Languages University (EFLU) — BA (Hons) English
*   **Course Title:** American Literature (Course Code: BAENGLITE355)
*   **Core Sub-Topics & Units:**
    *   **Unit I (Poetry):** Maya Angelou ("Phenomenal Woman", "Still I Rise"), Emily Dickinson, Walt Whitman.
    *   **Unit II (Drama):** Arthur Miller (Death of a Salesman); Tennessee Williams (The Glass Menagerie).
    *   **Unit III (Fiction):** Nathaniel Hawthorne (The Scarlet Letter); Ernest Hemingway (The Old Man and the Sea).

### 2. University of Hyderabad (UoH) — MA English
*   **Course Title:** Anglo-American Prose II
*   **Core Sub-Topics & Units:**
    *   **Unit I (Transcendentalism & Dark Romanticism):** Ralph Waldo Emerson, Nathaniel Hawthorne, Edgar Allan Poe, Herman Melville.
    *   **Unit II (Slave Narratives & Realism):** Harriet Jacobs (Incidents in the Life of a Slave Girl), Frederick Douglass (Narrative of the Life of Frederick Douglass), Mark Twain.
    *   **Unit III (Modernism & Post-war):** William Faulkner, Ernest Hemingway, Toni Morrison, Audre Lorde, Leslie Marmon Silko.
    *   **Prescribed Texts & Authors:** Toni Morrison (Beloved); Edgar Allan Poe ("The Fall of the House of Usher").

### NEP 2020 Structural Changes
American Literature has transitioned from a mandatory core requirement to a multi-track elective system (especially at the postgraduate level at UoH and JNU), allowing students to choose specialized sub-areas like "African-American Literature", "Jewish-American Literature", or "Native American Literatures".

## Module 3: Indian Writing in English (IWE)

### 1. University of Delhi (DU) — BA (Hons) English (UGCF)
*   **Course Title:** Indian Writing in English (Core DSC)
*   **Core Sub-Topics & Units:**
    *   **Unit I (Early Indian English Poetry):** Henry Louis Vivian Derozio ("To India - My Native Land"), Toru Dutt ("Our Casuarina Tree"), Sarojini Naidu.
    *   **Unit II (The Novel):** R.K. Narayan (Swami and Friends); Mulk Raj Anand (Untouchable).
    *   **Unit III (Post-Independence Drama & Poetry):** Nissim Ezekiel ("Night of the Scorpion"), Kamala Das ("An Introduction"), Jayanta Mahapatra.
*   **Prescribed Texts & Authors:** Anita Desai (In Custody); Mahesh Dattani (Tara).

### 2. University of Hyderabad (UoH) — MA English
*   **Course Title:** Dalit Literature (Core Paper)
*   **Core Sub-Topics & Units:**
    *   **Unit I (Theorizing Dalit Aesthetics):** Sharankumar Limbale (Towards an Aesthetic of Dalit Literature), Baburao Bagul.
    *   **Unit II (Poetry of Protest):** Namdeo Dhasal, L.S. Rokade, Challapalli Swaroopa Rani ("Water").
    *   **Unit III (Life Writing and Narratives):** Bama (Karukku), Omprakash Valmiki (Joothan).
*   **Prescribed Texts & Authors:** Arjun Dangle (Poisoned Bread); Baby Kamble (The Prisons We Broke).

### NEP 2020 Structural Changes
Under NEP 2020's Indian Knowledge Systems (IKS) mandate, Indian Writing in English has been significantly expanded and coupled with Indian Literature in Translation (Bhasha Literature). DU offers Generic Electives (GE) such as "Readings on Indian Diversities and Literary Movements" (GE-5) to expose non-English majors to translated bhasha texts.

## Module 4: World Literature

### 1. Jadavpur University (JU) — BA (Hons) English
*   **Course Title:** European Classical and Medieval Literature (Semester III, Course 3)
*   **Core Sub-Topics & Units:**
    *   **Unit I (Classical Epic):** Homer (The Iliad / The Odyssey).
    *   **Unit II (Classical Drama):** Sophocles (Oedipus the King); Euripides (Medea).
    *   **Unit III (Medieval European Epic):** Dante Alighieri (The Divine Comedy - Inferno).

### 2. University of Hyderabad (UoH) — MA English
*   **Course Title:** World Literature in Translation (Core Elective)
*   **Core Sub-Topics & Units:**
    *   **Unit I (European Modernism):** Franz Kafka (The Metamorphosis), Albert Camus (The Stranger).
    *   **Unit II (Latin American Boom):** Gabriel García Márquez (One Hundred Years of Solitude), Jorge Luis Borges ("Funes the Memorious").
    *   **Unit III (African Decolonization Narratives):** Ngũgĩ wa Thiong'o (Decolonising the Mind), Chinua Achebe (Things Fall Apart).

### NEP 2020 Structural Changes
In most active syllabi, World Literature has been decoupled from Eurocentric "Classical Literature" paradigms and integrated as a broader postcolonial elective paper, often titled "Postcolonial Literatures" or "Global Anglophone Literatures".

## Module 5: Literary Theory

### 1. University of Delhi (DU) — MA English
*   **Course Title:** Literary Theory (Core Paper)
*   **Core Sub-Topics & Units:**
    *   **Unit I (Structuralism & Post-Structuralism):** Ferdinand de Saussure ("Nature of the Linguistic Sign"), Roland Barthes ("The Death of the Author"), Jacques Derrida ("Structure, Sign and Play").
    *   **Unit II (Marxism & Ideology):** Karl Marx (The German Ideology), Louis Althusser ("Ideology and Ideological State Apparatuses"), Antonio Gramsci (Prison Notebooks).
    *   **Unit III (Psychoanalysis & Feminism):** Sigmund Freud, Jacques Lacan ("The Mirror Stage"), Judith Butler (Gender Trouble).
    *   **Unit IV (Postcolonialism & Race):** Edward Said (Orientalism), Gayatri Spivak ("Can the Subaltern Speak?"), Frantz Fanon (The Wretched of the Earth).

### 2. Jadavpur University (JU) — BA (Hons) English
*   **Course Title:** Literary Theory I & II (Semester VI, Course 14; Semester VIII, Course 22)
*   **Core Sub-Topics & Units:**
    *   **Unit I (Formalism to Structuralism):** Viktor Shklovsky ("Art as Technique"), Tzvetan Todorov.
    *   **Unit II (Post-Structuralism & Deconstruction):** Roland Barthes, Michel Foucault ("What is an Author?").
    *   **Unit III (Recent Developments):** Ecocriticism (Cheryll Glotfelty), Posthumanism (Cary Wolfe).

### NEP 2020 Structural Changes
Literary Theory has been pulled down from being exclusively an MA paper into the 3rd and 4th year of the FYUP (Semesters VI, VII, VIII). This structural shift ensures that students opting for a Research track at the UG level are equipped with theoretical tools to write their mandatory senior-year undergraduate dissertation.

## Module 6: Linguistics and ELT

### 1. University of Hyderabad (UoH) — Centre for English Language Studies (CELS) — MA English Language Studies
*   **Course Title:** Introduction to the Study of Language (EG 401)
*   **Core Sub-Topics & Units:**
    *   **Unit I (Foundations):** Language design features, animal vs. human communication, brain and language.
    *   **Unit II (Phonetics and Phonology - PEG 402):** Speech mechanism, English vowels and consonants, IPA transcription, syllable structure.
    *   **Unit III (Syntax and Morphology - EG 403):** Morphemes, word formation rules, immediate constituent analysis, Chomsky’s Transformational Generative Grammar.

### 2. English and Foreign Languages University (EFLU) — BA (Hons/Research) English
*   **Course Title:** Curriculum, Syllabus and Instructional Materials (CSIM)
*   **Core Sub-Topics & Units:**
    *   **Unit I (Syllabus Types):** Structural, Notional-Functional, Task-Based, and Content-Based syllabi.
    *   **Unit II (Materials Design):** Authenticity in language teaching materials, adaptation of literary texts for language teaching.
    *   **Unit III (Assessment):** Designing diagnostic, placement, and proficiency tests in English.

### NEP 2020 Structural Changes
Linguistics and ELT are highly favored under NEP 2020 as vocational and employable pathways. They are frequently structured as Skill Enhancement Courses (SECs) (e.g., "English Language Teaching" or "Linguistic Analysis") open to students from non-English streams.

## Module 7: Cultural Studies

### 1. University of Hyderabad (UoH) — MA English
*   **Course Title:** Introduction to Popular Culture (Elective Paper)
*   **Core Sub-Topics & Units:**
    *   **Unit I (Theorizing Pop Culture):** Theodor Adorno & Max Horkheimer ("The Culture Industry: Enlightenment as Mass Deception"), Walter Benjamin ("The Work of Art in the Age of Its Technological Reproducibility").
    *   **Unit II (Visual Culture and Cinema):** Semiotics of cinema, gaze theory (Laura Mulvey), and analysis of Indian popular cinema.
    *   **Unit III (Subcultures & Everyday Life):** Dick Hebdige (Subculture: The Meaning of Style), consumption, leisure, and cybercultures.

### 2. Jadavpur University (JU) — BA (Hons) / SWAYAM MOOC
*   **Course Title:** Evolution of Film Form and Digital Culture (Designed by Prof. Supriya Chaudhuri)
*   **Core Sub-Topics & Units:** Early Film Narration, Tom Gunning's "The Cinema of Attractions", transition from silent cinema to sound, Soviet montage theory, and contemporary digital visual cultures.

### NEP 2020 Structural Changes
Cultural Studies has moved from the periphery of English departments to the core of the curriculum. Under NEP 2020, "Culture Studies" is often offered as a Value Addition Course (VAC) or a Generic Elective (GE), connecting textual analysis with media literacy, film studies, and public culture.

## Module 8: Research Methodology

### 1. University of Delhi (DU) — MA English
*   **Course Title:** Introduction to Literary Research
*   **Core Sub-Topics & Units:**
    *   **Unit I (Methods in Literary Studies):** Gabriele Griffin (Research Methods for English Studies); Catherine Belsey ("Textual Analysis as a Research Method"); Carolyn Steedman ("Archival Methods").
    *   **Unit II (Digital Scholarship):** Database search parameters (JSTOR, Project MUSE), citation software, open-access protocols.
    *   **Unit III (Practical Drafting):** Formulating a research question, writing an annotated bibliography, drafting literature reviews.

### 2. University of Hyderabad (UoH) — MA English
*   **Course Title:** Research Methods (Course Instructor: Pramod K. Nayar)
*   **Core Sub-Topics & Units:**
    *   **Unit I (Epistemology of Research):** Research ethics, plagiarism check protocols, copyright laws.
    *   **Unit II (Textual Mechanics):** Working with primary manuscripts, archival records, physical vs. digital archives.
    *   **Unit III (Formatting):** In-depth application of the MLA Handbook (9th Edition) and Chicago Manual of Style.

### NEP 2020 Structural Changes
Under the FYUP 4-year model, Research Methodology is now mandatory in the 4th Year (Semester VII/VIII) for all students pursuing "BA Hons with Research". It acts as a prerequisite for the senior-year Undergraduate Dissertation (valued at 12 to 20 credits depending on the university).

## Module 9: Literary Criticism and Critical Approaches

### 1. University of Delhi (DU) — MA English
*   **Course Title:** Classical Literary Criticism
*   **Core Sub-Topics & Units:**
    *   **Unit I (Platonic/Aristotelian Debates):** Plato (Republic Book X), Aristotle (Poetics).
    *   **Unit II (Roman and Hellenistic):** Horace (Ars Poetica), Longinus (On the Sublime).
    *   **Unit III (Indian Aesthetics):** Bharata Muni (Natyashastra - "Rasa" and "Bhava" theories), Anandavardhana (Dhvanyaloka).

### 2. University of Delhi (DU) — MA English
*   **Course Title:** 16th-19th Century Literary Criticism
*   **Core Sub-Topics & Units:**
    *   **Unit I (Renaissance):** Philip Sidney (An Apology for Poetry).
    *   **Unit II (Romantic Defense):** Percy Bysshe Shelley (A Defence of Poetry).
    *   **Unit III (Victorian Functionalism):** Matthew Arnold (The Function of Criticism at the Present Time).

### NEP 2020 Structural Changes
While Literary Theory explores contemporary, post-1960s Continental philosophy, Literary Criticism has been kept as a foundational historical paper in the 3rd year (Semesters V & VI) across central universities to provide students with a historical lineage of western and eastern aesthetic philosophy.

## Module 10: English Language Proficiency

### 1. University of Delhi (DU) — BA (Hons) English (UGCF)
*   **Course Title:** Ability Enhancement Course (AEC): English (Language and Communication)
*   **Core Sub-Topics & Units:**
    *   **Unit I (Theory of Communication):** Types of communication (verbal, non-verbal), barriers to communication, active listening.
    *   **Unit II (Professional Writing):** Resume building, formal emails, writing business reports, and proposal drafting.
    *   **Unit III (Speaking Skills):** Public speaking, presentation design, and participating in Group Discussions (GD).

### 2. Jadavpur University (JU) — BA (Hons) English
*   **Course Title:** Academic Writing and Study Skills
*   **Core Sub-Topics & Units:**
    *   Paraphrasing vs. plagiarizing, synthesizing multiple sources, active reading of academic journal articles, and structuring persuasive essays.

### NEP 2020 Structural Changes
Under NEP 2020, English Language Proficiency is strictly categorized under Ability Enhancement Courses (AEC). It is no longer an internal elective of the English department but a mandatory credit requirement for all university undergraduates in their first year, standardized across Science, Commerce, and Arts streams.

## Module 11: Comparative Literature

### 1. Jadavpur University (JU) — Department of Comparative Literature
*(Note: Jadavpur is home to one of India's premier autonomous Departments of Comparative Literature, offering distinct courses separate from English)*.
*   **Course Title:** Thematology and Translation Studies
*   **Core Sub-Topics & Units:**
    *   **Unit I (Thematology in the Indian Context):** Swapan Majumdar ("Thematology in the Indian Context"), Sisir Kumar Das.
    *   **Unit II (Polysystem Theory):** Itamar Even-Zohar, Gideon Toury, Susan Bassnett.
    *   **Unit III (Comparing Regional Texts):** Comparative study of partition narratives across Bengali, Punjabi, and Urdu literatures (e.g., Saadat Hasan Manto, Sunil Gangopadhyay).

### 2. University of Hyderabad (UoH) — MA Comparative Literature
*   **Course Title:** Indian Literatures during the 19th Century (1800-1910)
*   **Core Sub-Topics & Units:**
    *   **Four Historical Time-Frames:** 1835 (English Education Act), 1857 (First War of Independence & rise of print), 1885 (Indian National Congress), and 1910 (Modern nationalisms).
    *   **Socio-Cultural Contexts:** Oral transmission, manuscript culture, colonialism, indigenous modernities, and translated English prose.

### NEP 2020 Structural Changes
NEP 2020 mandates the integration of regional Indian languages and literature into the mainstream. Comparative Literature has gained renewed prominence because of this. Rather than studying "Comparative Literature" as a specialized western discipline, it is increasingly taught under titles like "Bhasha Literatures of India in Translation".

## Module 12: Digital and New Media Literature

### 1. Jadavpur University (JU) — School of Cultural Texts and Records (SCTR)
*   **Course Title:** Post-graduate Certificate Course in Digital Humanities and Cultural Informatics
*   **Core Sub-Topics & Units:**
    *   **Unit I (Theoretical DH):** Methods and aims of Humanities in the digital era; new forms of digital textuality and on-screen reading.
    *   **Unit II (Practical Archiving):** Electronic archiving, preservation, and metadata design (Dublin Core schemas).

*   **Course Title:** Digital Humanities (Undergraduate level)
*   **Core Sub-Topics & Units:**
    *   **Unit I (Quantitative Literary Analysis):** Franco Moretti’s Graphs, Maps, Trees (distant reading vs. close reading).
    *   **Unit II (Computational Text Mining):** Word frequency distributions, topic modeling of literary texts.

### NEP 2020 Structural Changes
Digital Humanities is a major addition to NEP-designed curricula. It is introduced under Skill Enhancement Courses (SECs) (e.g., "Digital Content Writing" or "Methods in Digital Humanities") to bridge the humanities with computer applications, offering students vocational opportunities in digital archiving, tech industries, and publishing.

## Module 13: Professional Development of English Educators

### 1. English and Foreign Languages University (EFLU) — School of English Language Education (ELE)
*   **Course Title:** Teacher Development (Core MA ELE / PGDTE Paper)
*   **Core Sub-Topics & Units:**
    *   **Unit I (Reflective Teaching):** Action research in language classrooms, classroom observation methods, diary-writing, and peer-coaching.
    *   **Unit II (Psychology of Language Teaching):** Learner styles, motivation frameworks (Dornyei), and managing classroom dynamics in second-language setups.
    *   **Unit III (Continued Professional Development - CPD):** Professional teacher associations, designing teacher training workshops, and evaluating trainer-trainee programmes.

### 2. University of Hyderabad (UoH) — Centre for English Language Studies (CELS)
*   **Course Title:** Teaching Language and Literature (EG 553)
*   **Core Sub-Topics & Units:**
    *   **Unit I (Pedagogy of Grammar):** Deductive vs. inductive grammar teaching; task-based grammar lessons.
    *   **Unit II (Teaching Literature):** Stylistic approach to poetry, teaching short fiction and drama as language input.
    *   **Unit III (Micro-Teaching Practice):** Practical classroom teaching sessions, lesson plan defense, and continuous feedback loop.

### NEP 2020 Structural Changes
This module is largely integrated at the postgraduate or specialized diploma level (e.g., Post Graduate Diploma in Teaching English - PGDTE at EFLU) rather than as a general undergraduate degree paper. However, some universities are beginning to offer it as a Skill Enhancement Course (SEC) in the final semesters for students aiming directly at primary and secondary school education careers.
