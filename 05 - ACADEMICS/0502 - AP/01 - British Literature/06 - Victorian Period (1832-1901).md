# 6. Victorian Period (1832-1901)

## Historical and Cultural Context

### Political Events

-   **1832**: First Reform Act expands voting rights
-   **1837-1901**: Reign of Queen Victoria
-   **1838**: Beginning of Chartist Movement (working-class political reform)
-   **1839-1842**: First Opium War between Britain and China
-   **1845-1849**: Irish Potato Famine (Great Hunger)
-   **1846**: Repeal of the Corn Laws
-   **1848**: Year of Revolutions across Europe; Chartist demonstration in London
-   **1850**: Restoration of Roman Catholic hierarchy in England
-   **1851**: Great Exhibition at Crystal Palace
-   **1854-1856**: Crimean War
-   **1857-1858**: Indian Mutiny (First War of Indian Independence)
-   **1858**: Government of India Act transfers power from East India Company to British Crown
-   **1867**: Second Reform Act further expands voting rights
-   **1868**: Gladstone becomes Prime Minister for the first time
-   **1870**: Elementary Education Act establishes universal education
-   **1874**: Disraeli becomes Prime Minister for the second time
-   **1876**: Queen Victoria becomes Empress of India
-   **1880-1881**: First Boer War
-   **1884**: Third Reform Act extends franchise to rural workers
-   **1885-1886**: Scramble for Africa intensifies
-   **1893**: Independent Labour Party founded
-   **1899-1902**: Second Boer War
-   **1901**: Death of Queen Victoria; end of Victorian era
### Social and Cultural Developments

-   **1830s-1840s**: Railway boom transforms transportation and society
-   **1834**: New Poor Law establishes workhouses
-   **1837**: Victoria becomes queen at age 18
-   **1840**: Queen Victoria marries Prince Albert
-   **1840s**: "Hungry Forties" - period of economic depression
-   **1842**: Mines Act prohibits women and children working underground
-   **1843**: Charles Dickens publishes "A Christmas Carol"
-   **1847**: Factory Act limits working hours for women and children
-   **1848**: Pre-Raphaelite Brotherhood founded
-   **1851**: Census reveals more than half of Britain's population lives in cities
-   **1854**: Florence Nightingale leads nurses to Crimea
-   **1859**: Charles Darwin publishes "On the Origin of Species"
-   **1861**: Death of Prince Albert; Victoria enters prolonged mourning
-   **1863**: Football Association founded
-   **1870s**: "Great Depression" in British agriculture
-   **1870s-1890s**: Aesthetic Movement and Decadence
-   **1880s**: Rise of "New Woman" challenging Victorian gender norms
-   **1885**: Criminal Law Amendment Act criminalizes homosexuality
-   **1887**: Queen Victoria's Golden Jubilee
-   **1888**: Jack the Ripper murders in London
-   **1889**: London Dock Strike
-   **1891**: Thomas Cook organizes first package tour
-   **1895**: Oscar Wilde trials
-   **1897**: Queen Victoria's Diamond Jubilee
-   **1900**: Freud publishes "The Interpretation of Dreams"
### Literary and Artistic Milestones

-   **1836-1837**: Dickens's "Pickwick Papers" published in monthly installments
-   **1837-1838**: Dickens's "Oliver Twist"
-   **1847**: Charlotte Brontë's "Jane Eyre"; Emily Brontë's "Wuthering Heights"
-   **1848**: Thackeray's "Vanity Fair" completed
-   **1850**: Tennyson becomes Poet Laureate; Wordsworth dies
-   **1851-1853**: Ruskin's "The Stones of Venice"
-   **1853**: Charlotte Brontë's "Villette"
-   **1854**: Dickens's "Hard Times"
-   **1855**: Gaskell's "North and South"; Browning's "Men and Women"
-   **1857**: Flaubert's "Madame Bovary" (influential on English fiction)
-   **1859**: George Eliot's "Adam Bede"; Dickens's "A Tale of Two Cities"
-   **1860**: George Eliot's "The Mill on the Floss"
-   **1861**: Mrs. Beeton's "Book of Household Management"
-   **1862**: Christina Rossetti's "Goblin Market"
-   **1865**: Lewis Carroll's "Alice's Adventures in Wonderland"
-   **1866**: Dostoevsky's "Crime and Punishment" (influential on English fiction)
-   **1868**: Wilkie Collins's "The Moonstone"
-   **1871-1872**: George Eliot's "Middlemarch"
-   **1874**: Hardy's "Far from the Madding Crowd"
-   **1876**: George Eliot's "Daniel Deronda"
-   **1878**: Hardy's "The Return of the Native"
-   **1885**: Meredith's "Diana of the Crossways"
-   **1886**: Stevenson's "Dr. Jekyll and Mr. Hyde"
-   **1887**: Conan Doyle's first Sherlock Holmes story
-   **1890**: William Morris's "News from Nowhere"
-   **1891**: Hardy's "Tess of the d'Urbervilles"; Wilde's "The Picture of Dorian Gray"
-   **1895**: H.G. Wells's "The Time Machine"
-   **1896**: A.E. Housman's "A Shropshire Lad"
-   **1897**: Bram Stoker's "Dracula"; Wells's "The Invisible Man"
-   **1898**: Hardy's "Wessex Poems"
-   **1899**: Kate Chopin's "The Awakening"; Kipling's "Stalky & Co."
-   **1901**: Kipling's "Kim"
### Intellectual and Scientific Developments

-   **1830s-1840s**: Development of photography
-   **1833**: Faraday's laws of electrolysis
-   **1843**: John Stuart Mill's "System of Logic"
-   **1848**: Marx and Engels publish "The Communist Manifesto"
-   **1851**: Great Exhibition showcases industrial and technological achievements
-   **1854**: John Snow proves cholera is water-borne
-   **1855**: Wallace's paper on evolution
-   **1859**: Darwin's "On the Origin of Species"
-   **1860**: Oxford evolution debate between Huxley and Wilberforce
-   **1861**: Maxwell publishes theory of electromagnetism
-   **1865**: Mendel's work on genetics (rediscovered in 1900)
-   **1866**: First transatlantic telegraph cable successfully laid
-   **1867**: Marx's "Das Kapital" Volume 1
-   **1869**: Mendeleev's periodic table of elements
-   **1870**: Forster's Education Act establishes national education system
-   **1871**: Darwin's "The Descent of Man"
-   **1873**: Maxwell's "Treatise on Electricity and Magnetism"
-   **1876**: Bell patents telephone
-   **1877**: Edison invents phonograph
-   **1879**: Edison demonstrates incandescent light bulb
-   **1880s**: Emergence of eugenics movement
-   **1882**: Koch identifies tuberculosis bacterium
-   **1885**: Pasteur develops rabies vaccine
-   **1887**: Michelson-Morley experiment challenges Newtonian physics
-   **1895**: Röntgen discovers X-rays
-   **1896**: Becquerel discovers radioactivity
-   **1897**: J.J. Thomson discovers the electron
-   **1898**: Marie and Pierre Curie discover radium and polonium
-   **1899**: Freud's "The Interpretation of Dreams"
-   **1900**: Max Planck proposes quantum theory; Mendel's work rediscovered
