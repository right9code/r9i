# 3. Renaissance Period (1500-1660)

## Historical and Cultural Context

-   **1485-1603**: Tudor dynasty
-   **1517**: Protestant Reformation begins in Europe
-   **1534**: Henry VIII breaks with Roman Catholic Church
-   **1558-1603**: Elizabethan Age; English Renaissance flourishes
-   **1577-1580**: Francis Drake circumnavigates the globe
-   **1588**: Defeat of Spanish Armada
-   **1603-1625**: Jacobean Age under James I
-   **1605**: Gunpowder Plot
-   **1625-1649**: Caroline Age under Charles I
-   **1642-1651**: English Civil War
-   **1649**: Execution of Charles I
-   **1649-1660**: Commonwealth and Protectorate under Oliver Cromwell

### Tier 1: Essential Works and Authors

-   **William Shakespeare** (1564-1616)
    -   Tragedies: "Hamlet," "Macbeth," "King Lear," "Othello"
    -   Comedies: "A Midsummer Night's Dream," "Twelfth Night," "Much Ado About Nothing"
    -   Histories: "Henry V," "Richard III"
    -   Romances: "The Tempest," "The Winter's Tale"
    -   Sonnets (1609)
-   **Christopher Marlowe** (1564-1593)
    -   "Doctor Faustus," "Edward II," "The Jew of Malta"
-   **Edmund Spenser** (c. 1552-1599)
    -   "The Faerie Queene" (1590-1596)
-   **John Donne** (1572-1631)
    -   Metaphysical poetry, Holy Sonnets, sermons
-   **Ben Jonson** (1572-1637)
    -   "Volpone," "The Alchemist," lyric poetry
-   **Sir Thomas More** (1478-1535)
    -   "Utopia" (1516)

### Tier 2: Important Works and Authors

-   **Sir Philip Sidney** (1554-1586)
    -   "Astrophil and Stella," "The Defence of Poesy"
-   **Thomas Wyatt** (1503-1542) and **Henry Howard, Earl of Surrey**
    (1517-1547)
    -   Early sonneteers
-   **John Webster** (c. 1580-c. 1634)
    -   "The Duchess of Malfi," "The White Devil"
-   **Thomas Kyd** (1558-1594)
    -   "The Spanish Tragedy"
-   **George Herbert** (1593-1633)
    -   "The Temple"
-   **Andrew Marvell** (1621-1678)
    -   "To His Coy Mistress," political poetry
-   **John Milton** (1608-1674)
    -   "Paradise Lost" (1667), "Areopagitica" (1644)
-   **Robert Herrick** (1591-1674)
    -   "Hesperides"
