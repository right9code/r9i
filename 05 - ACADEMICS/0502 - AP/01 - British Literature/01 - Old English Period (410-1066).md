# 1. Old English Period (410-1066)

## Historical and Cultural Context

* **410**: Romans officially withdrew from Britain. 
* **449**: Traditional date for Anglo-Saxon invasion of Britain.
	* On invitation of Vortigern, Jutes (Hengist and Horsa) from Denmark (Jutland) arrived in Britain and fought the Picts.
	* **455**: Hengest and Horsa fought against Vortigern and claimed the kingdom of Kent.
		* Horsa was killed.
	* **Volkerwanderung**: The great migration of Germanic Tribes to England.
		* Soon More Tribes {Angles (south Denmark), Saxons (north Germany) and Jutes (Denmark)}came and established the Heptarchy.
			* Angles -->> ruled -->> Northumbria, Mercia and East Anglia
			* Saxon -->> ruled -->> Wessex, Essex, and Sussex
			* Jutes -->> ruled -->> Kent
		* Britons fled westwards (Wales, Southern Scotland, Cornwall and France) were called *Welsh* (strangers in Anglian terms.)
		* To themselves they called *Saxons* or *Engles* (now English.)
	* 
* **597**: St. Augustine converts Anglo-Saxons King Æthelberht of Kent to Christianity.
* **731**: Bede completes “Ecclesiastical History of the English People”
* **793**: First Major Viking Raid on Lindisfarne.
* **865-878**: The Great Heathen Army(Danish/Viking Army) invades Britain.
	* Led by **Ivar the Boneless**, Halfdan Ragnarsson, and Guthrum, the army included Danish and Norwegian warriors
* **871-899**: Reign of Alfred the Great, promoter of English literacy
	* **878**: Defeated Guthrum’s Viking army in Battle of Edington
		* leading to Treaty of Wedmore between **Wessex** (Alfred’s kingdom) and the **Danelaw** (Viking-controlled territories in eastern/northern England).
* **899-924**: Reign of Edward the Elder reconquered most of Viking Territory.
* **991**: Battle of Maldon fought against Vikings.
	* The English, led by Earl Byrhtnoth, were defeated, and the loss led to the payment of Danegeld (tribute) to the Vikings.
* **1016-1035**: Reign of Cnut (Danish rule of England)
	* Cnut the Great, a Danish King, conquered England, becoming king of Denmark, Norway and England. (the North Sea Empire) 
* **1042**: Anglo-Saxon monarchy is restored under Edward the Confessor as Danish line dies out.
	* the Danish royal line had died out with the death of Harthacnut.
	* After Edward’s father, Æthelred the Unready, died (1016), his mother Emma of Normandy married Cnut (1017), making Cnut Edward’s stepfather.
* **1066**: Norman Conquest; end of Anglo-Saxon England
	* **January:** Edward the Confessor dies; Harold Godwinson crowned king on 6 January.
		* Harold was the brother of Edith of Wessex, who was Edward’s wife, making Harold Edward’s brother-in-law.
		* Allegedly, Edward entrusted the kingdom to Harold and Edith shortly before his death
	* **September 25:** Battle of Stamford Bridge—Harold defeats Norwegian invaders led by Harald Hardrada and Tostig Godwinson.
	* **September 28:** William of Normandy lands in southern England.
		* William was the grandnephew of **Emma of Normandy**
		* **1064/1065**: William asserted that **Harold Godwinson** (Edward’s brother-in-law) had sworn an oath of loyalty to him during a trip to Normandy, possibly under threat.(allegedly)
	* **October 14:** Battle of Hastings—Harold is killed and William defeats the Anglo-Saxon army, ending Anglo-Saxon rule.
	* **December 25:** William the Conqueror crowned King of England at Westminster Abbey.

### Tier 1: Essential Works and Authors

-   **Beowulf** (c. 700-1000) - Epic poem combining pagan Germanic heroic code with Christian elements
	- Beowulf
	- Author: Anonymous
	- Hero: Beowulf, King of Geats, Son of Ecgtheow
	- Monster: Grendel
	- King: Hrothgar, King of Danes
	- Dialect: West Saxon
	- 3182 lines
	- Concludes with funeral ceremony of Beowulf.
	- First major poem in a European vernacular.
	- Seamus Heaney-translation-1999
-   **The Exeter Book** (c. 975) - Collection of Anglo-Saxon poetry including elegies and riddles
-   **Caedmon's Hymn** (c. 670) - Earliest recorded Old English poem
-   **The Dream of the Rood** (c. 8th century) - Christian vision poem
-   **The Wanderer** and **The Seafarer** - Elegiac poems exploring exile and transience
-   **Bede's Ecclesiastical History** (731) - Foundational historical text

### Tier 2: Important Works and Authors

-   **The Battle of Maldon** (c. 991) - Poem commemorating a historical battle against Vikings
-   **The Vercelli Book** (c. 10th century) - Collection of religious texts
-   **Alfred's translations** - Including Boethius's "Consolation of Philosophy"
-   **The Anglo-Saxon Chronicle** (c. 9th-12th centuries) - Historical annals
-   **Wulfstan's Sermon of the Wolf** (1014) - Apocalyptic sermon

### Tier 3: Supplementary Works and Authors

-   **The Wife's Lament** - Female-voiced elegiac poem
-   **The Junius Manuscript** - Biblical poetry
-   **Ælfric's Homilies** - Vernacular religious teachings
-   **Old English Riddles** - Poetic puzzles
-   **Waldere** (fragmentary) - Heroic poem
