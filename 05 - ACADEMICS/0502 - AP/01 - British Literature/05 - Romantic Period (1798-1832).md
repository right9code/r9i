# 5. Romantic Period (1798-1832)

## Historical and Cultural Context

###  Political Events

-   **1789**: French Revolution begins
-   **1793**: France declares war on Britain; Reign of Terror begins in
    France
-   **1798**: Irish Rebellion; Publication of Wordsworth and Coleridge's
    "Lyrical Ballads"
-   **1799**: Napoleon Bonaparte becomes First Consul of France
-   **1801**: Act of Union between Great Britain and Ireland
-   **1804**: Napoleon crowns himself Emperor
-   **1805**: Battle of Trafalgar; Nelson's victory and death
-   **1807**: Abolition of the slave trade in British Empire
-   **1811-1820**: Regency Period; George III declared insane, Prince of Wales becomes Regent
-   **1812**: War of 1812 between Britain and United States begins
-   **1814-1815**: Congress of Vienna; Reorganization of Europe after Napoleonic Wars
-   **1815**: Battle of Waterloo; Final defeat of Napoleon
-   **1819**: Peterloo Massacre in Manchester
-   **1820**: George IV becomes king
-   **1821-1832**: Greek War of Independence
-   **1829**: Catholic Emancipation Act
-   **1830**: July Revolution in France
-   **1832**: First Reform Act expands voting rights; End of Romantic period
### Social and Cultural Developments

-   **1798-1800**: Wordsworth and Coleridge publish "Lyrical Ballads"(considered the beginning of Romanticism)
-   **1802**: Wordsworth's preface to "Lyrical Ballads" (manifesto of Romantic poetics)
-   **1799-1803**: Agricultural Revolution accelerates
-   **1800-1830**: Industrial Revolution transforms British society and landscape
-   **1810s-1820s**: Gothic novel popularity peaks
-   **1811-1816**: Luddite movement protests against industrial machinery
-   **1814**: First steam locomotive
-   **1815**: Corn Laws enacted, raising food prices
-   **1816**: "Year Without a Summer" due to volcanic eruption; Mary Shelley begins writing "Frankenstein"
-   **1817**: Blackwood's Edinburgh Magazine founded
-   **1824**: National Gallery founded in London
-   **1825**: First passenger railway opens in England
-   **1828**: University College London founded (first secular university)
-   **1830**: Liverpool and Manchester Railway opens
### Literary and Artistic Milestones

-   **1798**: Wordsworth and Coleridge publish "Lyrical Ballads"
-   **1805**: Wordsworth completes first version of "The Prelude"
-   **1807**: Wordsworth's "Poems in Two Volumes"
-   **1809-1811**: Byron's "Childe Harold's Pilgrimage" (Cantos I and II)
-   **1810-1820**: Scott's historical novels published
-   **1812**: First volumes of Grimm's Fairy Tales published
-   **1813**: Jane Austen's "Pride and Prejudice" published
-   **1816**: Coleridge's "Christabel" and "Kubla Khan" published
-   **1817**: Keats's first volume of poems; Coleridge's "Biographia Literaria"
-   **1818**: Mary Shelley's "Frankenstein" published
-   **1819**: Keats's "Odes" written; Shelley's "Prometheus Unbound" and "The Cenci"
-   **1820**: Keats's final volume published; Shelley's "Prometheus Unbound" published
-   **1821**: Keats dies in Rome
-   **1822**: Shelley drowns in Italy
-   **1824**: Byron dies in Greece
-   **1824-1827**: Delacroix's Romantic paintings
-   **1830**: Alfred Tennyson's "Poems, Chiefly Lyrical" published
-   **1832**: Goethe dies; Scott dies
### Intellectual and Scientific Developments

-   **1798**: Malthus's "Essay on the Principle of Population"
-   **1802**: Establishment of Royal Institution for scientific research
-   **1807**: Hegel's "Phenomenology of Spirit"
-   **1809**: Lamarck's theory of evolution published
-   **1810**: Goethe's "Theory of Colors"
-   **1812**: Cuvier's "Researches on Fossil Bones"
-   **1817**: Ricardo's "Principles of Political Economy and Taxation"
-   **1818**: Mary Shelley's "Frankenstein" explores scientific ethics
-   **1820**: Hans Christian Ørsted discovers electromagnetism
-   **1821**: Faraday invents electric motor
-   **1827**: Ohm formulates his law of electrical resistance
-   **1830**: Charles Lyell's "Principles of Geology" challenges biblical chronology
-   **1831**: Faraday discovers electromagnetic induction; Darwin begins voyage on HMS Beagle
