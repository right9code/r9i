#  2. Middle English Period (1066-1500)

## Historical and Cultural Context

-   **1066**: Norman Conquest brings French influence to England
-   **1170**: Murder of Thomas Becket
-   **1215**: Magna Carta signed
-   **1300-1500**: Development of Middle English as literary language
-   **1337-1453**: Hundred Years' War between England and France
-   **1348-1350**: Black Death kills approximately one-third of England's population
-   **1381**: Peasants' Revolt
-   **1387-1400**: Chaucer writes "The Canterbury Tales"
-   **1399**: Henry IV deposes Richard II, beginning Lancaster dynasty
-   **1455-1485**: Wars of the Roses

### Tier 1: Essential Works and Authors

-   **Geoffrey Chaucer** (c. 1340-1400)
    -   "The Canterbury Tales" (1387-1400) - Collection of framed narratives showcasing medieval society
    -   "Troilus and Criseyde" (c. 1385) - Tragic romance set during Trojan War
-   **Sir Gawain and the Green Knight** (late 14th century) - Alliterative romance testing chivalric values
-   **William Langland's "Piers Plowman"** (c. 1370-1390) - Allegorical dream vision addressing social and religious issues
-   **Medieval Mystery Plays** - Biblical dramas performed by craft guilds
-   **"Everyman"** (c. 1500) - Morality play about salvation

### Tier 2: Important Works and Authors
