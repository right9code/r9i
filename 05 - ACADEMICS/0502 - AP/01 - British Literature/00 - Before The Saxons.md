# 0. Before The Saxons

- **Britons**: 
	- The earliest known inhabitants of the British Isles were Celtic-speaking peoples who inhabited the island of Great Britain akin to Gauls of rest of the Europe. They arrived to Britain in Iron Age (around 800 BC).

- **Romans**:
	- **55-54 BCE**: Julius Ceaser's unsuccessful campaign to colonise Britain.
		- Reasons: Poor preparation, bad storms, fierce local resistance, Rome's focus on Gaul, and Caesar's goal of gaining fame/insights (not permanent control).
	- **43-47 BCE**: A successful full-scale invasion under Emperor Claudius.
	- **122 AD**: Emperor Hadrian visits Britain, orders construction of Hadrian Wall (close to today's the border of England and Scotland).
		- 80 Roman miles from river Tyne to Solway Firth (1.48 kilometers or 0.92 modern miles)
		- The wall was built to mark the northern frontier of Roman Britain and to "separate the barbarians from the Romans"
	- **142 AD**: Under Emperor Antonious Pius; Romans built the Antonine Wall.
		- stretched from the Firth of Clyde at Old Kilpatrick to the Firth of Forth at Bo'ness, across central Scotland, serving as the new northern frontier of Roman Britain.
		- took about 6–12 years to complete.
	- 350 Years of Rule: Romans > Roman-Britons (Roman Father and Celtic Mother) > Britons (slaves*).
	- Picts and Scots (Irish raiders) frequently invaded Roman Britain.
	- **383 AD**: Romans withdrew their troops (Magnus Maximus claimed to Roman throne.)
		- The Roman general Magnus Maximus, who was then stationed in Britain, launched his bid for imperial power by withdrawing a significant portion of the Roman troops from northern and western Britain to support his campaign on the continent.
	- **410 AD**: Romans officially withdrew from Britain
		- **407 CE**: General Constantine III withdrew most remaining Roman troops from Britain to pursue his imperial ambitions in Gaul, leaving the island undefended.
		- **409–410 CE**: Romano-Britons expelled Constantine’s officials and rejected Roman governance, declaring independence due to Saxon raids and lack of imperial support.
		- **Honorius’ Rescript**: Emperor Honorius allegedly sent a letter in **410 CE** instructing British cities to “look to their own defense,” though some argue this may have been addressed to Bruttium (Italy), not Britain.
	- 446-54 AD: Britons seek help of Romans "Groans of the Britons" against the northern tribes but they were busy fighting Huns (Atila the Hun) attack.
