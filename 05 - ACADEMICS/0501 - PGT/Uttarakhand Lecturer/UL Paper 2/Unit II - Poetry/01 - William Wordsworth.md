# William Wordsworth


* **Key Terms Associated With**:
	* **Poetic Diction**: Wordsworth advocated for using the "real language of men" rather than artificial, elevated poetic language.
	* **Spontaneous overflow of powerful feelings**: His definition of poetry's origin.
	* **Emotion recollected in tranquility**: The process by which the spontaneous overflow is shaped into poetry.
		* This phrase from the *Preface to Lyrical Ballads*.
	* **Spots of Time**: Specific, vivid moments from childhood or youth, often connected to nature, that are deeply imprinted on memory and possess a regenerative power for the adult mind, helping to shape identity and poetic sensibility (a key concept in The Prelude).
	* **The Sublime (Wordsworth’s Approach)**:
		* The sublime arises from an interfusion of the self and the natural world
			* Unlike other poets (e.g., Shelley), who saw the sublime as something external and separate from humanity, Wordsworth envisioned a unity between the human mind and nature. 
			* As in _Tintern Abbey_: "A sense sublime of something far more deeply interfused, / Whose dwelling is the light of setting suns, / And the round ocean and the living air, / And the blue sky, and in the mind of man..."
## Lyrical Ballads (1798)

- Co-authored with Samuel Taylor Coleridge.
- Marked the beginning of English Romanticism.
- Important poems to focus on:
  - "Lines Written a Few Miles above Tintern Abbey"
  - "We are Seven"
  - "Simon Lee"
  - "The Thorn"
  - "The Idiot Boy"

## The Prelude (1850)

- An autobiographical epic poem.
- Chronicles the growth of Wordsworth’s mind and poetic spirit.
- Essential for understanding his philosophy of nature and poetry.

## Ode: Intimations of Immortality from Recollections of Early Childhood

- One of Wordsworth’s most celebrated odes.
- Explores themes of memory, childhood, and the loss of innocence.
- Famous for the line: "Our birth is but a sleep and a forgetting."

## The Solitary Reaper

- A lyric poem inspired by a Highland girl’s song.
- Celebrates nature, rural life, and the power of music.
- Frequently included in exam syllabi.

## The Excursion (1814)

- A long philosophical poem.
- Explores themes of nature, humanity, and society.
- Part of the larger, unfinished work "The Recluse."

## Poems in Two Volumes (1807)

- A significant poetry collection.
- Includes many of Wordsworth’s famous poems:
  - "Resolution and Independence"
  - "Ode: Intimations of Immortality"
  - "I Wandered Lonely as a Cloud" ("The Daffodils")
