```md
👀 What Your Eyes Say Without Words (ELI5)

1. Blink Rate = How often you blink
   • Blink a lot → nervous or stressed
   • Blink slowly/less → calm, curious, focused

2. Shutter Speed = How fast your eyelids move during ONE blink
   • Eyelids snap shut/open very fast → fear or panic (like a camera shutter!)
   • Eyelids move slow and smooth → calm and safe
   • Tip: Never move your eyes faster than you would in a swimming pool

3. Pupil Dilation (pupils get BIG)
   • Happens with excitement, interest, fear, or threat

4. Pupil Constriction (pupils get SMALL)
   • Happens when you see something unpleasant or uncomfortable

5. Confirmation Glances
   • Quick look at someone else while talking
   • Means you're checking: “Are they okay with this?”
   • Shows social status or need for reassurance

6. GHT (Gesture Hemispheric Tendency) – use only once near the end
   • Eyes, hands, and body tend to move to one side with positive feelings,
     and the other side with negative feelings

💡 Remember: Blinking = eyelids opening and closing.
   – Blink rate = how MANY times you blink
   – Shutter speed = how FAST each blink happens
```

The Platysma Muscle
The platysma is a broad, thin sheet of muscle located in the superficial fascia of the neck. 

    Location: It originates in the upper chest/shoulder region and extends upwards, inserting into the mandible (jawbone) and the skin/muscles around the lower part of the face.
    Function in Facial Expression: It tenses the skin of the neck and draws the corner of the mouth downward (a grimace or expression of fear/distress), matching the speaker's description of pulling the mouth back down.
    Protective Function: During a "fight-or-flight" response, the platysma tenses the neck, which may offer a minor, superficial layer of protection to underlying structures like the carotid arteries, though this is a less studied or primary function compared to its role in facial expression
