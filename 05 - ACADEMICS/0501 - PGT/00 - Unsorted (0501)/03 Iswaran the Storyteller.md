# Iswaran the Storyteller
**Author:** R.K. Laxman

## 🎯 Central Idea / Theme
*   **Art of Storytelling:** A good story is not just about the plot but how it is narrated (with expression and suspense).
*   **Superstition vs. Reality:** How fear can make us imagine things that aren't there.

## 👥 Key Characters
1.  **Mahendra:** A junior supervisor at construction sites. A bachelor with simple needs.
2.  **Iswaran:** Mahendra's cook. A master storyteller. Very loyal and resourceful.

## 📝 Quick Summary (Bullet Points)
*   **Iswaran's Talent:** He could weave endless stories and anecdotes. He was influenced by Tamil thrillers.
*   **Narrative Style:** He used dramatic gestures. Even for a small thing like a fallen tree, he would say, "There was a bushy beast lying across the road..."
*   **The Elephant Story:**
    *   A mad elephant escaped from the timber yard.
    *   It destroyed everything in the town.
    *   Iswaran (then a student) grabbed a cane, hit the elephant's third toenail, and it collapsed. (He claimed he used Japanese martial arts - Ju-Jitsu).
*   **The Ghost Story:**
    *   One night, Iswaran told Mahendra that their factory site was a burial ground.
    *   He claimed to see a "woman ghost holding a foetus" on full moon nights.
*   **The Effect:** Mahendra was scared. He started hearing sounds at night.
*   **The Climax:** One full moon night, Mahendra heard a low moan. He looked out and saw a dark cloudy form clutching a bundle. He broke into a cold sweat.
*   **The Next Morning:** Iswaran casually asked, "Sir, you saw the ghost last night, didn't you?"
*   **The End:** Mahendra was so terrified that he resigned from his job and left the haunted place immediately.

## 🔑 Important Quotes/Expressions
*   *"The story was narrated not in style but in a dramatic manner."*
*   *"Iswaran was an asset to Mahendra."*

## ❓ Critical Analysis (Exam POV)
*   **Was the ghost real?** Likely not. It was probably Mahendra's subconscious fear triggered by Iswaran's vivid storytelling.
*   **Humor:** The way Iswaran narrates the elephant incident is exaggerated and funny.
