# Detailed Notes: Inhabitants and Rulers of Britain (Including Early Human Species)

---

## Homo antecessor  
**Timeline:** c. 950,000–800,000 years ago  
- Considered among the earliest known human species to occupy Britain and much of Europe.  
- Fossil and stone tool evidence found in England (e.g., Happisburgh, Norfolk).  
- Possibly a common ancestor of modern humans and Neanderthals.  
- Lived during warm periods but populations fluctuated with climate changes and glaciations.  

---

## Homo heidelbergensis  
**Timeline:** c. 620,000–200,000 years ago  
- Successor to Homo antecessor in Britain, well-documented with fossils at Boxgrove, Sussex.  
- Considered a direct ancestor of Neanderthals and early modern humans.  
- Used more advanced stone tools, and demonstrated hunting activities.  
- Experienced repeated extinction and recolonization due to Ice Age glaciations.

---

## Homo neanderthalensis (Neanderthals)  
**Timeline:** c. 400,000–40,000 years ago  
- Earliest Neanderthal fossils in Britain found at Swanscombe Heritage Park, Kent.  
- Adapted to colder climates of later Ice Ages.  
- Known for tool use, burial practices, and possibly symbolic behavior.  
- Disappeared around 40,000 years ago, around the time anatomically modern humans arrived.

---

## Homo sapiens (Modern Humans)  
**Timeline:** From c. 40,000 years ago to present  
- First modern humans arrived c. 40,000 years ago with evidence from sites like Kent’s Cavern and Gough’s Cave.  
- Developed sophisticated tools, art, and culture.  
- Persisted through climatic fluctuations into the Mesolithic and later periods.  
- Modern Britons genetically descend partly from later migrations such as the Beaker people (~4,500 years ago).

---

## Celtic Britons  
**Timeline:** c. 700 BCE–43 CE  
- Iron Age tribal societies with Celtic languages and beliefs dominated Britain before Roman conquest.  
- Known for hillforts, metalwork, and Druids.

---

## Romans  
**Timeline:** 43–410 CE  
- Provincial rule brought infrastructure, cities, laws, and Christianity.

---

## Anglo-Saxons  
**Timeline:** c. 410–1066  
- Germanic tribes establish kingdoms and culture that form England.

---

## Vikings  
**Timeline:** Late 8th century–1066  
- Norse invasions and settlements influence language and culture.

---

## Normans  
**Timeline:** 1066–1154  
- Norman Conquest establishes new ruling class.

---

## Plantagenets  
**Timeline:** 1154–1485  
- Long-lasting medieval dynasty, including conflicts like the Hundred Years War.

---

## Tudors  
**Timeline:** 1485–1603  
- Renaissance and Reformation period under strong monarchs.

---

## Stuarts  
**Timeline:** 1603–1714  
- Union of crowns, civil war, and constitutional monarchy beginnings.

---

## Commonwealth/Protectorate  
**Timeline:** 1649–1660  
- Brief republic period.

---

## Hanoverians  
**Timeline:** 1714–1901  
- Rise of parliamentary government and empire expansion.

---

## Saxe-Coburg and Gotha / Windsor  
**Timeline:** 1901–present  
- Modern monarchy including current royal family.
