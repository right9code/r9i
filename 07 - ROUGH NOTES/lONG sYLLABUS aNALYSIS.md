# Curricular Architectures of English Studies in Indian Higher Education: A Comparative Institutional Audit under the NEP 2020 Framework

The structural landscape of English Studies in Indian higher education is undergoing a profound epistemological and structural transition. Driven by the mandates of the National Education Policy (NEP 2020), major public, central, and state universities are shifting away from historical Eurocentric canon-glorification toward an integrated, decolonial, and interdisciplinary model of literary and linguistic inquiry. This institutional audit evaluates how undergraduate (BA Hons) and postgraduate (MA) programs are structured across five representative target institutions: the University of Delhi (DU), the English and Foreign Languages University (EFLU) Hyderabad, Jawaharlal Nehru University (JNU) New Delhi, the University of Hyderabad (UoH), and Jadavpur University (JU) Kolkata.

To trace this transformation, this report systematically maps active university syllabi against thirteen core academic modules. It highlights specific course titles, precise unit and thematic breakdowns, prescribed primary and theoretical texts, and the integration of newer modules under NEP structural categories, including Discipline Specific Core (DSC), Discipline Specific Electives (DSE), Skill Enhancement Courses (SEC), Value Addition Courses (VAC), and Ability Enhancement Courses (AEC).

## Module 1: British Literature

British Literature continues to serve as a foundational pillar in Indian English Studies, but its pedagogical orientation has transitioned from a chronological survey to a critical interrogation of socio-political contexts, class relations, gender dynamics, and the ideological frameworks of imperialism.

### University of Delhi (DU)

Under the Undergraduate Curriculum Framework (UGCF) 2022, British Literature is divided into distinct thematic papers. At the undergraduate level, these include DSC-07: Romantic Literature and DSC-08: Victorian Literature. At the postgraduate (MA) level, the program is governed by the Post-Graduate Curriculum Framework (PGCF) 2024, which features a series of core papers titled Life, Literature and Culture (LLC).

- **Syllabus & Unit Breakdowns**:
    
    - _LLC I: Medieval Literature [Postgraduate]_: Unit I covers early representation and narrative verse, focusing on Marie de France’s _Lais_ ('Guigemar' and 'Equitan'), Thomas Malory’s _Morte d'Arthur_ (Books I and VII), and Geoffrey Chaucer’s 'Wife of Bath’s Prologue and Tale' and 'The Nun's Priest's Tale'. Unit II focuses on medieval theater, specifically the Wakefield Master’s _The Second Shepherd's Play_. Unit III covers allegorical pilgrimage through William Langland’s _Piers Plowman_. Unit IV examines medieval mysticism, gender, and devotion via the Letters of Abelard and Heloise, Andreas Capellanus’s _De Amore_, and _The Book of Margery Kempe_.
        
    - _ENG V: 16th and 17th Century Drama [Postgraduate]_: Unit I focuses on Shakespearean comedy, specifically _A Midsummer Night’s Dream_. Unit II explores early modern tragedy through William Shakespeare’s _Hamlet_. Unit III addresses political power, madness, and skepticism in William Shakespeare’s _King Lear_, studied alongside Michel de Montaigne’s philosophical essay "On Repentance". Unit IV investigates Jacobean satire and city comedy through Ben Jonson’s _Volpone_.
        

### English and Foreign Languages University (EFLU)

EFLU structures its postgraduate British Literature requirements across two comprehensive compulsory courses: Course III (British Literature I) and Course IV (British Literature II).

- **Syllabus & Unit Breakdowns**:
    
    - _Course III (British Literature I)_: Block I (Renaissance English Poetry including Milton) covers John Donne's "The Flea", "The Canonization", and "Holy Sonnets XIV", George Herbert's "The Collar", Andrew Marvell's "To His Coy Mistress", and John Milton's _Paradise Lost_ Book IX. Block II (Renaissance English Drama other than Shakespeare) prescribes Ben Jonson's _Volpone_, Christopher Marlowe's _Edward II_, Thomas Kyd's _The Spanish Tragedy_, and John Webster's _The Duchess of Malfi_. Block III (Shakespeare) centers on _As You Like It_, _Othello_, _Henry V_, and _The Tempest_. Block IV (Augustan Poetry and Drama) analyzes John Dryden's "MacFlecknoe", Alexander Pope's "The Rape of the Lock", William Wycherley's _The Country Wife_, and William Congreve's _The Way of the World_. Block V (Rise of the British Novel) includes Samuel Richardson's _Pamela_, Daniel Defoe's _Robinson Crusoe_, Henry Fielding's _Joseph Andrews_, and Jonathan Swift's _Gulliver’s Travels_.
        
    - _Course IV (British Literature II)_: Block I (Romantic and Victorian Poetry) includes William Wordsworth's "Tintern Abbey" and "Ode: Intimations of Immortality", S.T. Coleridge's "Rime of the Ancient Mariner", P.B. Shelley's "Ode to the West Wind", John Keats's "Ode to a Nightingale", and poems by Robert Browning, Alfred Lord Tennyson, Matthew Arnold, and G.M. Hopkins. Block II (The Nineteenth-Century Novel) features Jane Austen's _Emma_, Emily Brontë's _Wuthering Heights_, Charles Dickens's _Hard Times_, and Thomas Hardy's _Tess of the d’Urbervilles_. Block III (Twentieth-Century Poetry) studies Thomas Hardy, Edward Thomas, Philip Larkin, T.S. Eliot's "The Love Song of J. Alfred Prufrock", and W.B. Yeats. Block IV (Twentieth-Century Drama) covers G.B. Shaw's _Arms and the Man_, John Osborne's _Look Back in Anger_, and Samuel Beckett's _Waiting for Godot_. Block V (Twentieth-Century Novel) covers Joseph Conrad's _Lord Jim_, D.H. Lawrence's _The Rainbow_, and James Joyce's _A Portrait of the Artist as a Young Man_.
        

### Jadavpur University (JU)

Jadavpur University’s state-level curriculum frames British Literature chronologically at the undergraduate level and offers specialized research clusters at the postgraduate level.

- **Syllabus & Unit Breakdowns**:
    
    - _Major 1: The Romantic Period [Undergraduate]_: The poetry unit contrasts canonical male poets (Wordsworth’s Lucy poems, Coleridge’s _Ancient Mariner_, Byron’s _Beppo_, Keats’s "Ode on a Grecian Urn", Shelley’s _Masque of Anarchy_) with female writers like Charlotte Smith and Anna Barbauld. The prose unit studies Thomas Paine’s _Rights of Man_, Mary Wollstonecraft’s _A Vindication of the Rights of Woman_, Mary Shelley’s _Frankenstein_, and Thomas Love Peacock’s _Nightmare Abbey_.
        
    - _Major 2: The Victorian Period [Undergraduate]_: Unit I (Fiction) selects three novels and two short stories from authors like Elizabeth Gaskell, W.M. Thackeray, Charles Dickens, George Eliot, and Thomas Hardy. Unit II (Poetry) studies Elizabeth Barrett Browning, Edward Fitzgerald, Alfred Tennyson, Robert Browning, and Christina Rossetti. Unit III (Non-fiction) focuses on Thomas Carlyle, Henry Mayhew, John Ruskin, and Walter Pater.
        
    - _Postgraduate Clusters_: Advanced electives are organized into specialized tracks. Cluster A includes _The Pilgrimage in Medieval Literature_ (Eng/PG/A4) and _Revenge Tragedy_ (Eng/PG/A18). Cluster B features _Society and Literature during the English Civil War_ (Eng/PG/B1). Cluster D focuses on _Darwin and the Victorian Novel_ (Eng/PG/D5).
        

### University of Hyderabad (UoH)

UoH offers specialized postgraduate seminars that examine early modern and long-eighteenth-century developments.

- **Syllabus & Unit Breakdowns**:
    
    - _Renaissance Literature and Thought_: This course explores themes of race, gender, politics, and religion. Readings include Shakespeare’s _Hamlet_ and selected sonnets (12 and 20), Christopher Marlowe’s _Doctor Faustus_, Ben Jonson's "To Heaven", Thomas Hobbes’s _Leviathan_ (excerpts on "Of Sense" and "Of Imagination"), and gender-focused early modern poetry by Amelia Lanyer and Mary Wroth.
        
    - _Eighteenth Century English Literature and Thought_: This paper addresses satire, class, and gender. It covers Jonathan Swift’s _A Modest Proposal_, essays by Joseph Addison and Richard Steele, Alexander Pope’s _An Essay on Criticism_, John Locke’s _Two Treatises of Government_, and Mary Astell’s feminist tract _A Preface, in Answer to Some Objections to Reflections upon Marriage_.
        

## Module 2: American Literature

American Literature modules explore the development of the American literary voice, analyzing the tensions between mainstream myths—such as the American Dream, frontier expansion, and transcendentalism—and marginalized critiques of race, gender, and displacement.

```
                  AMERICAN LITERATURE COMPREHENSIVE MAPPING
                  
      [19th Century Foundations]  -->  [The Modernist Divide]  -->  [Contemporary & Subaltern]
      - Transcendentalism (Emerson)    - Domestic Tragedy (Miller)   - Slave Narratives (Douglass)
      - American Gothic (Hawthorne)    - Post-War Realism (Wilson)   - Decolonial Indigeneity (Alexie)
      (UoH, JU Cluster F3)             (EFLU Drama, UoH MA)          (DU DSC-10, JU Cluster F5)
```

### University of Delhi (DU)

- **Course Titles & Levels**: DSC-10: American Literature (BA Hons, Semester IV); ENG XXIII: Literatures of the Americas (MA Elective).
    
- **Syllabus & Unit Breakdowns**:
    
    - _DSC-10 (American Literature) [Undergraduate]_: Unit I focuses on race, memory, and trauma in Toni Morrison’s _Beloved_. Unit II examines gender identity and corporate alienation in Kate Chopin’s _The Awakening_ and Herman Melville’s 'Bartleby, the Scrivener'. Unit III analyzes democratic ideals and indigenous displacement, studying Walt Whitman’s 'O Captain, My Captain' and 'Passage to India' (lines 1–68) alongside Sherman Alexie’s poetry, including 'Crow Testament' and 'Evolution'.
        
    - _Secondary Readings & Contexts_: Suggestive readings include Ralph Waldo Emerson’s 'Self-Reliance', Martin Luther King Jr.’s 'I Have a Dream' speech, Frederick Douglass's _Narrative of the Life of Frederick Douglass_ (Chapters 1–7), Adrienne Rich’s feminist essay 'When We Dead Awaken: Writing as Re-Vision', and J. Hector St. John de Crèvecoeur’s _Letters from an American Farmer_.
        

### University of Hyderabad (UoH)

- **Course Titles & Levels**: American Poetry, Fiction and Drama / Anglo-American Prose (MA Elective/Core tracks).
    
- **Syllabus & Unit Breakdowns**:
    
    - _American Poetry, Fiction and Drama_: Unit I (Short Fiction) analyzes Washington Irving’s "Rip Van Winkle", Nathaniel Hawthorne’s "Rappaccini's Daughter", and Herman Melville’s "Bartleby, the Scrivener". Unit II (Novels) compares Mark Twain’s _The Adventures of Huckleberry Finn_, Kate Chopin’s _The Awakening_, and Toni Morrison’s _The Bluest Eye_. Unit III (Drama) contrasts the puritanical critiques of Arthur Miller’s _The Crucible_ with the racial politics of August Wilson’s _Fences_.
        

### Jadavpur University (JU)

- **Course Titles & Levels**: Major 11: American Literature (BA Hons, Semester V); Cluster F: Elective Papers Eng/PG/F3 (American Literature in the Nineteenth Century), Eng/PG/F4 (American Literature in the Twentieth Century), and Eng/PG/F5 (Ethnic-American Literature).
    
- **Syllabus & Unit Breakdowns**:
    
    - _Eng/PG/F5 (Ethnic-American Literature) [Postgraduate]_: Focuses on non-canonical narratives, exploring the slave narrative tradition, Chicano border literature, Asian-American migration histories, and the Native American Renaissance.
        
    - _Eng/UG/O19 (Edgar Allan Poe) [Undergraduate Option]_: A dedicated single-author course analyzing gothic architecture, psychological terror, and the origins of detective fiction in works like "The Fall of the House of Usher" and "The Murders in the Rue Morgue".

IWE curricula trace the historical trajectory of English writing in India. These modules examine how a colonial linguistic tool was adapted to express nationalist movements, post-independence disillusionment, regional identities, and anti-caste subaltern resistance.

### Curricular Analysis

At JNU, the curriculum focuses on an "Indo-Centric Approach to Literary Studies," prioritizing translation and marginality. In ES440E (Dalit Studies in India), the program analyzes how Dalit life-writing challenges traditional English aesthetics.

At DU, the undergraduate DSC-11 syllabus pairs canonical writers like Anita Desai and Salman Rushdie with postcolonial critiques by Meenakshi Mukherjee ("Divided by a Common Language") and Raja Rao's _Foreword to Kanthapura_. This structure encourages students to examine the linguistic variations and cultural contexts of English in India.

## Module 4: World Literature

World Literature modules shift the focus of comparative literary studies away from Eurocentric models. By analyzing postcolonial literatures from the Global South alongside classical Western texts, these courses explore shared historical contexts of colonialism, migration, and anti-imperial resistance.

```
                      WORLD LITERATURE CURRICULAR RANGE
                                       
      [Western Classical Roots]  -->  [Postcolonial Global South]  -->  [Dystopia & Speculative]
      - Greek Tragedy (Sophocles)     - African Decolonization (Achebe)  - Climate Dystopias (Bradbury)
      - Roman Epic (Virgil)           - Caribbean Resistance (Walcott)   - Post-human Futures (Wells)
      (DU DSC-02, JU Major 3)         (DHWU, JU Cluster F6/F7)           (UoH Elective, DU GE)
```

### University of Delhi (DU)

- **Course Titles & Levels**: DSC-02: European Classical Literature (BA Hons); GE-03: Dystopian Writings (BA Hons Generic Elective).
    
- **Syllabus & Unit Breakdowns**:
    
    - _DSC-02 (European Classical Literature) [Undergraduate]_: Unit I covers early epic poetry through Homer’s _The Odyssey_ (specifically studying narrative structure and classical heroism). Unit II introduces classical dramaturgy, studying Sophocles’s _Antigone_ alongside Aristotle’s theorization of tragedy in the _Poetics_. Unit III focuses on classical political comedy through Aristophanes’s _Lysistrata_.
        
    - _GE-03 (Dystopian Writings) [Undergraduate]_: Unit I covers Mary Shelley’s _The Last Man_. Unit II analyzes scientific anxiety and temporal travel in H.G. Wells’s _The Time Machine_. Unit III focuses on authoritarianism and state control in Ray Bradbury’s _Fahrenheit 451_ (studied alongside critical essays by Erika Gottlieb and Adam Stock).
        

### Jadavpur University (JU)

- **Course Titles & Levels**: Major 3: European Classical and Medieval Literature (BA Hons, Semester III); Major 19: World Literature in Translation (BA Hons, Semester VII); Cluster F: Eng/PG/F6 (African Writing in English) and Eng/PG/F7 (Caribbean Writing in English).
    
- **Syllabus & Unit Breakdowns**:
    
    - _Major 3 (European Classical and Medieval Literature)_: Module I (Classical Greek and Roman) introduces Mediterranean cultures, Mycenaean societies, the rise of Rome, and epic/lyric traditions through Homer’s _The Iliad_ or _Odyssey_, Virgil’s _Aeneid_, tragedies of Aeschylus, comedies of Plautus (_Pot of Gold_), and selections from Sappho, Catullus, and Horace. Module II (Medieval European) studies late antiquity, the rise of scholasticism, and vernacular epics. It includes _Beowulf_, the _Song of Roland_, Middle English romances (_Gawain and the Green Knight_), and late medieval poetry by Geoffrey Chaucer, William Langland, and Dante.
        

### University of Hyderabad (UoH)

- **Course Titles & Levels**: Introduction to World Literature (BA Hons, Semester III Core DSC 4); In Other Worlds: An Introduction to Contemporary Dystopian Fiction (MA Elective).
    
- **Syllabus & Unit Breakdowns**:
    
    - _In Other Worlds: Contemporary Dystopian Fiction_: Focuses on Anglo-American and postcolonial dystopian fiction from the mid-20th to the early 21st century. It studies how power dynamics (class, race, gender), state governmentality, and technological advancement are represented. Short story readings include Ray Bradbury’s "A Sound of Thunder" and "There Will Come Soft Rains", Kurt Vonnegut’s "Harrison Bergeron", Shirley Jackson’s "The Lottery", and Ursula K. Le Guin’s "The Ones Who Walk Away From Omelas".
        

## Module 5: Literary Theory

Literary Theory has transitioned from an elective study of structuralist and formalist systems to an active critique of power. Curricula across public, central, and state universities investigate postcoloniality, gender theory, psychoanalysis, subaltern histories, and critical disability studies.

|**Postgraduate Curricular Mapping of Literary Theory**|||
|---|---|---|
|**University**|**Course Title & Classification**|**Key Sub-Topics / Units Covered**|
|**EFLU**|Course II (Compulsory): Literary Criticism and Theory (Part II Core).|Blocks III & IV: 20th Century Criticism and Theory, mapping the transition from New Criticism to post-structuralism and postcolonial theory.|
|**DU**|ENG VII: Postcolonial Literatures and Theory (Core); ENG XIV: Criticism and Theory II (Elective).|Epistemological decolonization, Subalternity, Orientalism, gender performativity, and Marxist aesthetics.|
|**JU**|Major 14: Literary Theory I (BA Hons); Major 22: Literary Theory II (BA Hons); Cluster G (MA electives).|Post-structuralism, Deconstruction, Psychoanalysis (Eng/PG/G4), Foucault and Discourse (Eng/PG/G3), and diverse Feminist Theories (Eng/PG/G2).|
|**UoH**|Advanced Critical Theories (MA Core/Elective tracks).|Post-marxism, Frankfurt School, eco-criticism, affect theory, and post-humanism.|

### Curricular Analysis

At Jadavpur University, undergraduate students must complete a two-part sequence: Literary Theory I (Semester VI) and Literary Theory II (Semester VIII). This pathway is complemented by Major 18 (Feminist and Queer Studies), which explores Judith Butler’s theories of gender performativity and Michel Foucault’s _The History of Sexuality_.

At the postgraduate level, JU’s Cluster G offers specialized seminars on single theorists, notably Eng/PG/G3 (Foucault) and Eng/PG/G12 (Introduction to Psychoanalysis). This specialized framework prepares students for the conceptual demands of contemporary academic research.

## Module 6: Linguistics and ELT

The integration of Linguistics and English Language Teaching (ELT) has moved away from prescriptive grammar instruction toward descriptive linguistics, sociolinguistics, second language acquisition (SLA) theories, and localized language teaching methodologies.

```
                     LINGUISTICS AND ELT CURRICULAR ENGINE
                                        
      [Theoretical Foundations]  -->  [Sociolinguistics & SLA]  -->  [Syllabus & Material Design]
      - IPA Transcription             - Langue / Parole (Saussure)   - Task-Based Material Design
      - Phrase Structure Grammar      - Bilingualism (Multilingual)  - Language Testing/Evaluation
      (EFLU LS-111, UoH PEG 402)      (UoH EG 451, EFLU ELE 221)     (UoH EG 454, EFLU ELE 141)
```

### English and Foreign Languages University (EFLU)

As a premier institution for language studies, EFLU offers two distinct postgraduate pathways: the MA in Linguistics and the MA in English Language Teaching (ELT).

- **MA Linguistics Core Papers**: LS-111: Phonetics I, LS-121: Phonology I, LS-131: Morphology I, and LS-141: Syntax I.
    
- **MA ELT Core Papers**: Organized into five specific research areas: Area 1 (English in India) includes ELE 111 (ELT in India). Area 2 (Perspectives on Teaching/Learning) features ELE 121 (Approaches to Second Language Teaching) and ELE 221 (Theories of SLA). Area 3 (The Second Language Classroom) features ELE 131 (The Teaching of Skills) and ELE 330 (Practicum). Area 4 (Syllabus and Materials) includes ELE 141 (Syllabus Design) and ELE 243 (Designing Materials). Area 5 (ELT Research) contains ELE 251 (An Introduction to Research Methodology in ELT).
    

### University of Hyderabad (UoH - Centre for English Language Studies - CELS)

The Centre for English Language Studies (CELS) offers a dedicated MA in English Language Studies, structured around formal linguistics and applied educational theories.

- **Syllabus & Unit Breakdowns**:
    
    - _EG 401 (Introduction to the Study of Language) [Postgraduate]_: Unit I covers fundamental concepts, including Ferdinand de Saussure’s theories of Langue vs. Parole, Paradigmatic vs. Syntagmatic relations, and Synchronic vs. Diachronic studies, alongside language properties and genetic classifications. Unit II (Language and Communication) explores communicative competence, gesture and sign languages, speech vs. writing systems, and language games. Unit III (Key Thinkers) contrasts Leonard Bloomfield's American Structuralism, Noam Chomsky's Mentalist Theory of Grammar/Universal Grammar, and M.A.K. Halliday's systemic functional grammar, alongside Ludwig Wittgenstein and the Sapir-Whorf hypothesis. Unit IV traces the history of the English language, analyzing phonological, orthographic, syntactic, and semantic shifts. Unit V addresses applied fields of study and language learning.
        
    - _PEG 402 (Phonetics and Phonology) [Postgraduate]_: Focuses on phonetic description, IPA transcription, acoustic properties of speech sounds, phonemic analysis, neutralization, assimilation, and suprasegmental features like word stress, sentence intonation, and stress shifts.
        

## Module 7: Cultural Studies

Cultural Studies modules allow universities to study non-canonical cultural texts and systems. These programs analyze low culture, mass media, visuality, body politics, and identity, shifting focus away from high literary aesthetics toward ideological critiques of daily life.

### University of Hyderabad (UoH)

- **Course Titles & Levels**: MAEN401DST: Introduction to Cultural Studies (MA, Semester IV Core); FN128: Introduction to Folk Culture Studies (MA Foundation Course).
    
- **Syllabus & Unit Breakdowns**:
    
    - _MAEN401DST (Introduction to Cultural Studies)_: Unit I focuses on the foundations of cultural theory (exploring the contributions of the Birmingham School, Stuart Hall, and Raymond Williams). Unit II studies hegemony, ideology, and popular culture. Unit III analyzes subcultures, visual culture, consumerism, and representations of identity across mass media.
        
    - _FN128 (Introduction to Folk Culture Studies)_: Unit I addresses the conceptual definitions of folk culture and oral traditions. Unit II focuses on material folk culture, performance arts, and performance aesthetics. Unit III examines the impact of globalization, copyright, and preservation policies on indigenous folk traditions.
        

### Jadavpur University (JU)

- **Course Titles & Levels**: MA Research Areas: Textuality and Culture, and Cultural Transactions (CAS Phase I, II, III thrust areas); MA Special Electives: Eng/PG/H5 (Popular Literature), Eng/PG/H2 (History of the Book), Eng/PG/H8 (Queer Studies), and Eng/PG/H13 (Literature and Film).
    
- **Syllabus & Unit Breakdowns**:
    
    - _Eng/PG/H2 (History of the Book)_: Focuses on the transition from manuscript culture to print culture in colonial India. It covers the development of local printing presses, colonial publishing history (specifically Oxford University Press in India), and paper manufacturing, studied alongside the CAS publications _Print Areas_ and _Moveable Type_.
        

### University of Delhi (DU)

- **Course Titles & Levels**: ENG VIII: Disability Studies and Literary Representations (MA Core); ENG XVI: Visual Studies (MA Open Elective); ENG XXVII: Violence and Memory Studies (MA Open Elective).
    
- **Syllabus & Unit Breakdowns**:
    
    - _ENG XXVII (Violence and Memory Studies)_: Unit I covers partition trauma, historical displacement, and memory. Unit II studies the representation of political violence in visual arts and memoirs. Unit III explores oral history methodologies and the construction of national identity.
        

## Module 8: Research Methodology

With the implementation of NEP 2020, Research Methodology has transitioned from a postgraduate elective to a key component of the undergraduate curriculum. These papers prepare students for their fourth-year honors thesis, focusing on research ethics, digital indexing, and humanities research methods.

### Curricular Analysis

At the state-university level, Jadavpur University integrates analytical bibliography and text preservation directly into its research training. This approach is supported by the Centre for Advanced Study (CAS) focus on print culture and the history of the book.

At the central-university level, Delhi University’s postgraduate ENG XV course focuses on technical training. It guides students through the mechanics of academic writing, draft preparation, and the intellectual property guidelines mandated by the University Grants Commission (UGC).

## Module 9: Literary Criticism and Critical Approaches

Literary Criticism is a core curricular component that bridges classical aesthetic systems with early 20th-century Western movements. Indian universities are increasingly adopting a comparative model that places classical Sanskrit poetic treatises alongside Western classical and modern texts.

```
                     COMPARATIVE AESTHETICS FRAMEWORK
                     
      [Indian Rasa / Dhvani]        <-- [Conceptual Dialogue] -->      [Western Classical / Modern]
      - Bhartrhari (Syntax/Meaning)                                   - Plato (Mimesis & Republic X)
      - Anandavardhana (Dhvani)                                       - Aristotle (Catharsis & Poetics)
      - Kuntaka (Vakrokti-Jivita)                                     - Arnold (Touchstone Method)
      (DU ENG VI, JNU ES647E)                                         (EFLU Block I, JU Major 14)
```

### University of Delhi (DU)

- **Course Titles & Levels**: DSC-09: Literary Criticism (BA Hons, Semester III); ENG VI: Criticism and Theory I (MA Core, Semester II).
    
- **Syllabus & Unit Breakdowns**:
    
    - _ENG VI (Criticism and Theory I) [Postgraduate]_:
        
        - **Unit I (Indian Aesthetics)**: Studies Bhartrhari’s "On Syntax and Meaning" (from _Vakyapadiya_), Anandavardhana’s "Dhwani: Structure of Poetic Meaning" (from _Dhvanyaloka_), and Kuntaka’s "Language of Poetry and Metaphor" (from _Vakrokti-Jivita_).
            
        - **Unit II (Classical Western)**: Contrasts Plato’s critique of mimesis in _The Republic_ (Book X) with Aristotle’s defence of tragic representation in the _Poetics_.
            
        - **Unit III (Romantic to Victorian)**: Evaluates Philip Sidney’s _An Apology for Poetry_, P.B. Shelley’s "A Defence of Poetry", and Matthew Arnold’s "The Study of Poetry".
            
        - **Unit IV (Formalism & New Criticism)**: Studies I.A. Richards's "Metaphor" and "The Command of Metaphor", Cleanth Brooks's "The Formalist Critics", and Northrop Frye’s "The Archetypes of Literature".
            
    - _DSC-09 (Literary Criticism) [Undergraduate]_: Unit I covers David Hume’s "Of the Standard of Taste". Unit II analyzes the aesthetic category of the sublime in Edmund Burke’s _A Philosophical Enquiry into the Origin of Our Ideas of the Sublime and Beautiful_ (specifically Part 1: Sections VII and XVIII; Part 2: Sections I–VIII; and Part 3).
        

### Jawaharlal Nehru University (JNU)

- **Course Titles & Levels**: ES474E: Texts in Literary Criticism; ES647E: Indian Poetics: The Theory of Dhvani; ES650E: Vakyapadiya: Philosophy of Language and Linguistics.
    
- **Syllabus & Unit Breakdowns**:
    
    - _ES650E (Vakyapadiya: Philosophy of Language and Linguistics) [Postgraduate]_: Focuses on the philosophy of Bhartrhari. It covers semantic units, sentence meaning, the concept of _sphota_ (flashing forth of meaning), and linguistic ontology, providing a classical Indian model for analyzing meaning systems.
        

## Module 10: English Language Proficiency

Under the NEP 2020 framework, English Language Proficiency modules have been redesigned. No longer limited to remedial or passive grammar instruction, these papers are integrated into the curriculum as Credit-bearing Ability Enhancement Courses (AEC) and Skill Enhancement Courses (SEC) that develop functional, academic, and professional communication skills.

### English and Foreign Languages University (EFLU)

### Comparative Observations

State universities, such as Jadavpur University, frame Comparative Literature within regional print histories and local adaptations, supported by research projects like "Shakespeare in Bengal" and "Literary Ecology in Nineteenth-Century Bengal".

Central universities, like Delhi University's Department of Modern Indian Languages and Literary Studies, structure their programs around a pan-Indian comparative network. DU's program studies reception histories, Bhakti networks, and Tribal Literatures (CILEC 403) across multiple languages, including Assamese, Bengali, Gujarati, Kannada, Marathi, Tamil, and Telugu.

### Curricular Analysis

### Decolonial Reconstruction of Literary Theory and Criticism

Central and state universities are shifting away from a purely Western theoretical model toward a comparative, decolonial approach. This is evident in the integration of Sanskrit aesthetic treatises (_Rasa Shastra_, _Dhvani_ theory, and _Vakrokti-Jivita_) alongside Western classical poetics in the core papers of Delhi University, Jawaharlal Nehru University, and the University of Hyderabad.

By studying Bhartrhari, Anandavardhana, and Kuntaka in conversation with Plato, Aristotle, and New Criticism, these programs encourage students to critically evaluate Western critical terms through Indian aesthetic frameworks.

### Applied Linguistics and Professionalization

Linguistics and ELT have transitioned from abstract theories to applied, professional skills. This is particularly clear in EFLU’s MA ELT and UoH’s MA English Language Studies, which prioritize classroom practicums, syllabus design, materials development, and testing methodologies over theoretical language models.

This applied focus prepares students for career paths in educational administration, curriculum design, and language training.

### Vocational Integration under the NEP 2020 Framework

The introduction of the Undergraduate Curriculum Framework (UGCF) has modularized English programs, allowing students to combine core literary studies with vocational Skill Enhancement Courses (SEC) and Value Addition Courses (VAC).

Students can combine core studies in British and American literature with practical courses in _Digital Humanities_, _Editing and Copywriting_, _Translation in Practice_, and _Film Studies_. This multi-disciplinary structure bridges the gap between academic literary analysis and practical career pathways in digital publishing, copy-editing, and content curation.
