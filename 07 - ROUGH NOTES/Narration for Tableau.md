
"Before the mind can truly bloom, the broken soil must first be healed."

Our Venerable Mother Seraphina , by heart and training, was a teacher.
But history did call her to be more. 
In 1908, when the Messina-Reggio earthquake shattered thousands of lives, she did not just stopped at offer prayers; 
Her deep compassion compelled her to open Welfare Centres to rehabilitate the orphaned children left behind.

This tableau, **"The Healing Heart of a Teacher,"** presents a hospital centre inspired by the noble mission of Mother Seraphina, where service to humanity is the highest duty. Here, we see patients from poor and underprivileged backgrounds who have come seeking treatment, care, and hope. Many of them cannot afford expensive medical facilities, yet they are welcomed here with open hearts.

Inspired by Mother’s courage during the First World War—where her sisters served as front-line nurses— with selfless love serving each and  every patient with love.

They see no difference of caste, nor the religion, nor the status—only the human being of need.

Every wound tended with patience, 
Every pain is heard with kindness.

In this sacred space, it is not only the wounds of the flesh that are healed, but also the deeper wounds of the spirit — the neglects of respect, of dignity, of emotional support.

From Italy to Romania, India and Brazil and to the whole world **CFMSS** has reached to people where no one else could. 

A kind word, a gentle touch,  a caring smile becomes the most powerful medicine here.
This tableau reminds us that true service lies in helping those who are suffering in mind and in body. 

As we look upon **"The Healing Heart of a Teacher,"** let us carry her motto, _"Porto i Misteri"_—I Carry the Mysteries—and pledge to serve our world with the same compassion, selflessness, and love.
